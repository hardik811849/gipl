GIPL has won 9th eINDIA Award for

Innovative use of ICT

It is a matter of proud and prestige for all of us that GIPL has won

9th eINDIA Award for Innovative use of ICT. There were about 284

nominations out of which GIPL was considered and adjudged one

of the deserving contestant for this award by the jury consisting

eminent personalities from the industry and government. GIPL has

developed a Tablet base software application for Gujarat Agriculture

department for implementation of “Talavadi (Pond) Subsidy

scheme”. With the help of this software the periphery & Photo of

Pond will be depicted along with the survey number and Latitude

& Longitude through GPS and the photo will be uploaded at

Central Server through GPRS on line. This award was presented

at the 9th eINDIA Conference held on23-24th July, 2013 at

Hyderabad International Convention Centre, Hyderabad. Honorable

Chief Minister of AP, Shri N. Kiran Kumar Reddy, was the Chief

Guest and Shri K.S. Rao, Minister for Textile, Govt of India, Mr

Ponnala Lakshmaiah, Minister- IT, Govt of AP and

Mr. J. Satyanarayana Secretary- DeITy Govt of India were \

Guest of Honour. The award was presented by: Shri.Ponnala

Lakshmaiah Minister-IT, Gov. of Andhra Pradesh) Shri.P K Mohanty,

IAS (Chief Secretary, Gov. of Andhra Pradesh) Sanjay Jaju

(Secretary, Information Technology & Communications Dept., Gov

of Andhra Pradesh)

GIPL Launched Live E-Darshan Service

GIPL is successfully providing live streaming (E – Darshan) of four

major Hindu temples located across Gujarat.

 

AMBAJI: Ambaji is an important temple town with millions of

devotees visiting the Ambaji temple every year. It is one of the

51 Shaktipeeths. . The Temple of Ambaji is recognized as one of the

original Shakti Pithas (religious texts) where, according to the

ancient Scriptures, the heart of the goddess Ambaji fell to earth

when her body was dismembered. DAKOR: Dakor in Kheda District

of Gujarat is prominent for its grand temple of Shree Krishna which

have a historical significance. Dakor Krishna is known Ranchhodrai.

It is said that he ran away from battle when Kalyavan attacked him

as an ally of Jarasandh. So Krishna is called Ranchhodrai - one who

ran away from the battle. SOMNATH: The Someshwar Mahadev

temple stands tall among the temples of India. The construction of

the present temple in Junagadh district began in 1950. It is the

seventh temple built to commemorate the glory of Lord Somnath

who was known as Bhairaveshwar. The pinnacle rides to a height o

155 feet. The kalash atop the shikhar weighs 10 tonnes. The flag-

mast is 37 feet long. DWARKA: Dwarka (Jamnagar district) in

ancient Anarta (Saurashtra) was the capital of Lord Krishna\\\'s

terrestrial kingdom. The Dwarkadhish temple (also known as Jagat

temple) and its Sikhar rises to 170 feet. The pataka or flag of the

temple is changed three times a day. The five-storied temple stands

on 60 pillars. The pilgrims enter the temple by Swarg Dwar (the

gateway of heaven) and leave by Moksh Dwar (the gateway of

salvation).The temple has rich carvings It is one of the important

moksh dhams. Please visit the website http://purplestream.com /

edarshan/
